'New Post: Import an OVF from your desktop to a #vCloud Org Catalog #PowerCLI' publicize_results: a:1:{s:7:"twitter";a:1:{i:14061017;a:2:{s:7:"user_id";s:12:"jakerobinson";s:7:"post_id";s:18:"198075192323481602";}}} _wpas_done_twitter: '1' --- Have you downloaded a really cool appliance in OVF and want to quickly upload it to your vCloud Director Org? The Web UI would be one way to do it, but I sometimes run into issues with the java uploader. Here's a simple PowerCLI method to get your OVF appliance from your desktop into your Org: [sourcecode language="powershell"] $ovfPath = "C:\users\me\desktop\MyCoolAppliance.ovf" $orgVdc = Get-OrgVdc "My Org vDC" $catalog = Get-Catalog "My Catalog" Import-CIVAppTemplate -SourcePath $ovfPath -OrgVdc $orgVdc -Catalog $catalog [/sourcecode] As a subjective observation, it seems to run a lot faster than the Java uploader, but pending a real speed test, it's just another way to upload an OVF into your catalog. :D
